Most people try to change their life by starting with strategy first.

They ask:

- “What should I do?”
- “What plan should I follow?”
- “What steps will solve this problem?”
- “How do I overcome this challenge?”

So the default order most people follow is:
- Strategy
- Story
- State

But in reality, lasting change usually works in the opposite direction. Because:
- your strategy is shaped by your story,
- and your story is shaped by your state.

So if your state is:
- fearful,
- exhausted,
- hopeless,
- overwhelmed,
- emotionally defeated,

then even good strategies often fail because:
- the mind interprets reality negatively,
- possibilities seem invisible,
- action feels heavy,
- and the future appears doomed.

The more natural order of transformation is (aligned with how human beings actually function in reality ; the path of lower internal resistance and higher psychological alignment):
- State — change the condition you are operating from
- Story — change the meaning and interpretation generated from that condition
- Strategy — change the actions and systems that emerge from that new perspective
