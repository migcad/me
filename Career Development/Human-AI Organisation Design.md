# Human-AI Organisation Design

**Intentional design of human-AI interactions** is critical, as organizations that prioritize this see **twice the likelihood** of exceeding AI ROI expectations and **2.5 times** better financial results compared to those that do not. Most firms currently fail by layering AI onto legacy human-designed workflows, whereas successful designs treat AI as a primary executor requiring **ground-up process redesign** rather than simple automation.

Effective organizational design relies on **fluidity**, defined as **agility multiplied by trust**, enabling organizations to cycle between structured execution and collaborative problem-solving. This requires **hardwiring** (clear roles, accountability, and escalation protocols) and **softwiring** (psychological safety and leadership behaviors) to support **human-AI collaboration** where humans remain accountable for AI agency.

Key structural shifts include moving from hierarchical, human-optimized workflows to **fluid, skill-based organizations** where humans act as **orchestrators** or governors. Emerging roles like **Human-AI Collaboration Designers** and **AI Orchestrators** are essential to manage **multi-AI coordination**, define **decision boundaries**, and ensure **human-centered** outcomes that augment rather than replace human judgment.
