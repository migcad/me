These roles fall into several major domains.


# The highest leverage meta-role

Stepping back, many of these roles converge on a broader capability that could become one of the most strategically valuable in large enterprises:

**Enterprise Reinvention Architect** (or **AI-Native Enterprise Architect**).

This person operates across strategy, business architecture, operating model design, organisational design, AI systems, governance, economics, and change management. Rather than optimizing isolated functions, they redesign how the enterprise creates value in an environment where intelligence is abundant, software is increasingly autonomous, and humans focus on the highest-value judgment, creativity, and relationship work.

Key responsibilities include:

- Defining the target AI-native operating model.
- Redesigning value streams, business capabilities, and organisational structures.
- Allocating work across humans, AI agents, software, and physical automation.
- Designing governance, risk controls, and accountability for autonomous systems.
- Building reusable enterprise capabilities rather than isolated AI solutions.
- Orchestrating large-scale organisational transformation and adoption.

## The common thread

Across all of these roles, the scarce skill is **enterprise systems thinking**, not merely AI tool proficiency. As foundation models and AI engineering become increasingly commoditized, competitive advantage is likely to come from people who can answer questions such as:

- What should this enterprise look like if AI were assumed from day one?
- How should work, decisions, knowledge, and authority be redistributed across humans and intelligent systems?
- Which parts of the organisation remain differentiated, and which become shared AI capabilities?
- How can the enterprise continuously adapt its structure as AI capabilities evolve?

In other words, the next wave of hiring is likely to shift from **building AI systems** to **designing AI-native enterprises**. Technical AI expertise will remain essential, but it will increasingly be combined with business architecture, organisational design, economics, governance, and transformation leadership. Those who can integrate these disciplines are likely to occupy some of the highest-impact and highest-demand positions over the coming decade.
