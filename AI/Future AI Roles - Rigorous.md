# Taxonomy of High-Demand Roles for AI-Native Enterprise and Institutional Reinvention

## 0. Purpose and Design Constraints of This Taxonomy

This taxonomy is designed to classify roles that are likely to be in high demand across:

- Governments
- For-profit enterprises
- Non-profit organisations

as they transition from:

> “Using AI to optimise existing processes”

to:

> “Redesigning operating models, organisational structures, and value creation systems around AI-native capabilities”

### Design properties enforced

This taxonomy is constructed to satisfy the following constraints:

- **Exhaustive coverage** of enterprise reinvention work
- **Orthogonality** across role categories (minimal conceptual overlap)
- **Mutual exclusivity** of role responsibilities at the category level
- **Functional distinctiveness** (each role class performs a unique system function)
- **Ontological uniformity** (consistent definition style across roles)
- **Ontological fidelity** (roles map to real organisational functions, not abstractions only)
- **Balanced granularity** (neither overly atomic nor overly aggregated)
- **Scalability** (supports sub-roles and specialisations)
- **Minimality** (no redundant categories)
- **Interpretability** (clear meaning for organisational adoption)

# 1. Top-Level Ontology of Enterprise Reinvention Work

All roles in AI-native transformation fall into **six fundamental functional domains**:

```text
1. Strategic Direction Design
2. Operating Model Design
3. Capability & System Design
4. Workforce & Organisation Design
5. Governance & Risk Design
6. Value Realisation & Adaptation Design
```

Each domain is mutually exclusive in *primary function*, but collectively exhaustive for enterprise reinvention.

# 2. Full Role Taxonomy

## 2.1 Strategic Direction Design Roles

These roles define **what the organisation should become** in an AI-native environment.

### 2.1.1 Enterprise Futures Architect

**Function:**
Designs long-term organisational evolution under AI-driven economic conditions.

**Core responsibilities:**
- Defines future business or mission directions
- Models AI-driven industry structure changes
- Identifies obsolete and emerging value chains
- Designs multi-year transformation trajectories

**Distinctiveness:**
Focuses on *future states*, not current systems or implementation.

---

### 2.1.2 AI Competitive Strategy Designer

**Function:**
Designs competitive positioning using AI as a structural advantage.

**Core responsibilities:**
- Identifies AI-based competitive asymmetries
- Designs differentiation strategies in AI-saturated markets
- Determines where AI creates monopoly-like advantages
- Maps competitor capability displacement

**Distinctiveness:**
Focuses on *external competitive dynamics*, not internal redesign.

---

### 2.1.3 Institutional Mission Reframer (Public & Non-Profit Specific)

**Function:**
Redefines institutional purpose under AI-augmented service delivery conditions.

**Core responsibilities:**
- Reframes public service delivery models
- Reinterprets mission effectiveness in AI contexts
- Aligns policy goals with AI-enabled execution capacity

**Distinctiveness:**
Focuses on *mission legitimacy and purpose*, not efficiency.

---

## 2.2 Operating Model Design Roles

These roles define **how the organisation functions structurally and operationally**.

### 2.2.1 AI-Native Operating Model Designer

**Function:**
Designs the end-to-end organisational operating system under AI integration.

**Core responsibilities:**
- Redesigns workflows across the enterprise
- Defines AI-human interaction structures
- Eliminates redundant operational layers
- Defines decision pathways and automation boundaries

**Distinctiveness:**
Focuses on *system-wide operating structure*, not individual processes.

---

### 2.2.2 Enterprise Process Decomposition Architect

**Function:**
Deconstructs legacy processes into modular, AI-executable components.

**Core responsibilities:**
- Breaks processes into atomic decision units
- Identifies automation candidates
- Reconstructs workflows into AI-native pipelines

**Distinctiveness:**
Focuses on *process decomposition*, not system design.

---

### 2.2.3 Decision Systems Architect

**Function:**
Designs how decisions are made across the organisation.

**Core responsibilities:**
- Maps decision rights (human vs AI)
- Designs decision automation thresholds
- Implements decision traceability systems

**Distinctiveness:**
Focuses only on *decision-making structures*, not execution workflows.

---

## 2.3 Capability & System Design Roles

These roles define **what the organisation can do as reusable capabilities**.

### 2.3.1 Enterprise Capability Architect

**Function:**
Defines modular enterprise capabilities as reusable assets.

**Core responsibilities:**
- Builds capability maps across the enterprise
- Converts functions into reusable services
- Standardises capability interfaces

**Distinctiveness:**
Focuses on *capabilities as assets*, not workflows or organisation.

---

### 2.3.2 AI Systems Integration Architect

**Function:**
Integrates AI systems into enterprise-wide capability structures.

**Core responsibilities:**
- Connects AI models to enterprise systems
- Designs multi-system AI orchestration
- Ensures interoperability of AI agents and services

**Distinctiveness:**
Focuses on *technical and logical integration*, not business structure.

---

### 2.3.3 Enterprise Knowledge Systems Architect

**Function:**
Designs organisational knowledge as structured, machine-usable systems.

**Core responsibilities:**
- Converts tacit knowledge into structured knowledge
- Builds organisational memory systems
- Designs retrieval and reasoning infrastructure

**Distinctiveness:**
Focuses on *knowledge representation*, not execution or strategy.

---

## 2.4 Workforce & Organisation Design Roles

These roles define **how humans, AI, and automation collaborate**.

### 2.4.1 Human-AI Workforce Designer

**Function:**
Designs hybrid human-AI labour systems.

**Core responsibilities:**
- Allocates tasks across humans, AI, and automation
- Defines augmentation vs replacement boundaries
- Designs collaborative workflows

**Distinctiveness:**
Focuses on *labour allocation*, not organisational structure.

---

### 2.4.2 Organisational Topology Designer

**Function:**
Designs organisational structure as a dynamic system.

**Core responsibilities:**
- Defines hierarchy flattening or expansion
- Designs network-based organisations
- Optimises structural latency and coordination cost

**Distinctiveness:**
Focuses on *structure shape*, not roles or capabilities.

---

### 2.4.3 Digital Workforce Operations Manager

**Function:**
Manages AI agents and automated systems as operational workforce units.

**Core responsibilities:**
- Monitors AI agent performance
- Manages AI lifecycle (deployment, updates, retirement)
- Ensures reliability of digital workforce

**Distinctiveness:**
Focuses on *ongoing operations*, not design.

---

## 2.5 Governance & Risk Design Roles

These roles define **control, accountability, and safety systems**.

### 2.5.1 AI Governance Architect

**Function:**
Designs governance systems for AI-enabled decision-making.

**Core responsibilities:**
- Defines AI accountability structures
- Establishes auditability frameworks
- Sets approval hierarchies for AI decisions

**Distinctiveness:**
Focuses on *control systems*, not operational execution.

---

### 2.5.2 Algorithmic Risk Officer

**Function:**
Manages systemic risks arising from AI and automation.

**Core responsibilities:**
- Identifies model and system risks
- Monitors bias, drift, and failure modes
- Designs mitigation frameworks

**Distinctiveness:**
Focuses on *risk identification and mitigation*, not governance structure design.

---

### 2.5.3 Institutional Compliance Architect

**Function:**
Ensures regulatory and ethical compliance in AI-enabled systems.

**Core responsibilities:**
- Translates regulation into system constraints
- Embeds compliance into workflows
- Maintains audit readiness

**Distinctiveness:**
Focuses on *external compliance alignment*, not internal governance.

---

Focuses on *value measurement*, not system design.

**Function:**
Designs organisations as continuously evolving systems.

**Core responsibilities:**
- Embeds continuous improvement loops
- Designs adaptive operating models
- Enables perpetual restructuring capability

**Distinctiveness:**
Focuses on *continuous change systems*, not static transformation.

---

### 2.6.3 Enterprise Learning Systems Designer

**Function:**
Designs how organisations learn and adapt over time.

**Core responsibilities:**
- Builds feedback loops across systems
- Enables organisational learning from AI outputs
- Improves decision systems over time

**Distinctiveness:**
Focuses on *learning dynamics*, not execution or governance.

**Distinctiveness:**

# 4. Structural Summary of Mutual Exclusivity

Each domain is mutually exclusive by primary function:

- Strategy → Defines direction
- Operating Model → Defines structure of work
- Capability Design → Defines reusable systems
- Workforce Design → Defines labour allocation
- Governance → Defines control and risk
- Value Realisation → Defines outcomes and adaptation

Cross-domain roles exist only for coordination, not duplication.

> **multi-layer enterprise systems design under conditions of machine intelligence abundance**
