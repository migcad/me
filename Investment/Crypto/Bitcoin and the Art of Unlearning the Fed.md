# Bitcoin and the Art of Unlearning the Fed
Jul 7 2026, Jordi Visser

## Summary
The article argues that Bitcoin may be approaching a turning point despite widespread bearish sentiment.\
It contends that the forces that have weighed on Bitcoin over the past eight months—particularly the rise of agentic AI—may soon become catalysts for its recovery rather than continued weakness.

## Key points
- Extremely negative sentiment can signal a bottom.\
The author argues that markets often begin recovering before the narrative changes, once selling pressure is exhausted.
- AI has indirectly hurt Bitcoin.\
The rise of advanced AI systems shifted investor capital toward AI infrastructure (chips, data centers, power, cooling, etc.) and away from crypto. This also strengthened expectations of higher interest rates, which pressured Bitcoin.
- Two major catalysts could reverse Bitcoin's fortunes:\
Capital rotation: As AI infrastructure stocks become crowded and harder to generate outsized returns from, investors may seek new opportunities. Bitcoin could become an attractive alternative tied to the broader AI transformation without being part of the crowded AI equity trade.\
Changing interest-rate expectations: If investors begin viewing AI primarily as a productivity-enhancing force rather than an inflationary one, expectations for aggressive rate hikes could ease, benefiting Bitcoin.
- The market is mispricing AI's economic effects.\
According to the author, investors focus too heavily on AI's demand-side effects (massive investment in infrastructure) while underestimating its supply-side effects (digital labor, automation, and productivity gains).
Higher productivity could reduce inflationary pressures even as economic output remains strong.
- Central banks may need a new framework.
Citing former policymaker Kevin Warsh, the piece argues AI represents a fundamental shift that traditional "hawkish vs. dovish" monetary policy frameworks may not adequately capture. Productivity gains from AI could change how the Federal Reserve responds to growth and inflation.
- Bitcoin could benefit from the emerging AI economy.\
As autonomous AI agents become more common, they may require neutral financial infrastructure for settlement, authentication, and value transfer. While not every AI transaction would use Bitcoin, the author believes a world of autonomous software increases the value of scarce, decentralized monetary assets.
- Fiscal deficits strengthen Bitcoin's long-term case.\
Even if AI boosts productivity, persistent government deficits and rising debt remain structural issues. Bitcoin is presented as an asset that could benefit in an environment where governments rely on debt and central banks face increasingly difficult policy tradeoffs.

## Overall thesis
The article concludes that Bitcoin's next bull market may not be driven simply by expectations of lower interest rates. Instead, it could emerge from a broader shift in how investors understand AI's impact on productivity, monetary policy, and the financial system. If markets "unlearn" the traditional Fed narrative and recognize AI's disinflationary and productivity-enhancing effects, Bitcoin could benefit from both improving macro conditions and its role as a scarce digital asset in an AI-driven economy.
